Julian Siravo is the founder and editor of Winethropocene. An American-Italian architect and urban designer trained in London, he co-directs the Autonomy Institute, where he runs the Public Futures Lab, and co-founded the design practice Rowhill Studio in 2015. His research runs across cities, climate, care, and the logistics of food — which is how he ended up reading the climate crisis through the one crop that has kept written records for seven thousand years.
