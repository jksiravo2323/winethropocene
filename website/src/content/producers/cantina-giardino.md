Cantina Giardino was founded by a group of friends in Irpinia, determined to preserve the region's old, often-abandoned vineyards and indigenous grape varieties. They work with growers across the region, paying premium prices for high-quality fruit from old vines on volcanic soils.

The wines are made with minimal intervention — spontaneous fermentations, no fining or filtration, and little to no sulphur. The result is a collection of wines that speak clearly of place and vintage. Their Aglianico, in particular, has become a benchmark for the variety's potential in Campania.

In the context of climate change, Irpinia's altitude (300–700m) and volcanic soils provide a natural buffer against rising temperatures, making the region an increasingly important reference point for southern Italian viticulture.
