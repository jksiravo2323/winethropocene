## Overview

The Andes snowpack and glacier system is the primary water source for the wine regions of western Argentina and central Chile. Snow that falls on the high Andes melts in spring and summer, feeding the rivers that irrigate vineyards in Mendoza, San Juan, and the Chilean Central Valley.

That system is shrinking. The snowpack has declined by roughly 30% since 1990. Glaciers are retreating at accelerating rates — some have lost more than half their mass since the mid-20th century. And the meltwater that remains is arriving earlier in the season, creating a timing mismatch with the period of peak irrigation demand.
