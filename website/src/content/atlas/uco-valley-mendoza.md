## Overview

The Uco Valley is Argentina's premier high-altitude wine region, where Malbec grown at 900–1,500 metres above sea level produces wines of uncommon intensity and freshness. The region's elevation, intense sunlight, and wide diurnal temperature range create a unique terroir that has made Argentine Malbec a global brand.

But the Uco Valley depends almost entirely on irrigation from Andes snowmelt. The snowpack has declined by roughly 30% since 1990. The glaciers that feed Mendoza's rivers are retreating at accelerating rates. Multi-year drought — now in its fourth consecutive year — is testing the region's viability.
