## Overview

England is one of the clearest winners of the poleward shift in wine-growing suitability. Acreage has quadrupled in 20 years to approximately 4,000 hectares. English sparkling wine now competes with — and frequently beats — Champagne in international blind tastings. And for the first time, the climate window for still red wine is opening.

## The Numbers

English wine production has grown from a curiosity in the 1970s to a serious industry. The chalk soils of southern England — the same geological formation that surfaces in Champagne — combined with a warming climate, have created conditions remarkably similar to Champagne's historical norms. The result: sparkling wines with the high acidity and mineral tension that define the category.
