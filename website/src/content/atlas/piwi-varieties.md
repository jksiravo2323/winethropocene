## Overview

PIWI — from the German *Pilzwiderstandsfähig*, "fungus-resistant" — varieties are hybrid grapes bred by crossing European *Vitis vinifera* with American and Asian *Vitis* species that carry natural resistance to fungal diseases. The result: grapes that need 70–90% fewer fungicide treatments than traditional European varieties.

For decades, PIWI varieties were confined to marginal regions and organic niches. That is changing. As disease pressure rises with a warmer, wetter climate — and as EU regulations tighten on fungicide use — PIWI varieties are moving toward the mainstream. Bordeaux and Champagne have both approved experimental PIWI plantings. Denmark and Sweden have built their industries on them.

## The Breakthrough

The quality of PIWI wines has improved dramatically in the past decade. Early varieties produced wines with "foxy" flavours inherited from their American parentage. Modern PIWI varieties — developed through multi-generational backcrossing — have largely eliminated those characters, producing wines that can stand alongside traditional European varieties in blind tastings.
