## Overview

Denmark's commercial wine industry barely existed in 2000. Today it has approximately 3,500 hectares of vineyard — larger than England's — and is growing rapidly. Cold-hardy PIWI varieties like Solaris and Rondo dominate, but classic varieties like Pinot Noir are becoming viable as temperatures rise.

In 2023, a Danish producer released the country's first commercial still red wine from Pinot Noir. It sold out within weeks. Denmark is no longer a curiosity — it is a preview of the northern wine frontier.
